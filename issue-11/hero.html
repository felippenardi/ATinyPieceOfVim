Use ctrl+a and ctrl+x to easily increment and decrement a number in Vim.

Issue #11

Have you ever had to retype a number just to increment or decrement it by a certain amount?

Vim has a simple command in Normal Mode for adding and subtracting the next number after the cursor by one unit: ctrl+a and ctrl+x.

This commands really shines when you precede it with the number of times you want it to repeat.

To increment the next number after the cursor by 10, you just type 10 ctrl+a.

To decrement it by 22, you just type 22 ctrl+x.

Needless to say, you can repeat the increment/decrement by just pressing "." after you've done it the for first time.



Remaping keys to play nicely Tmux

If you want to remap the key bind because of Tmux, or for any other reason, you can do it easily.

Here is what you need to add to your .vimrc in order to achieve this:

" Easily increase and decrease next number with ctrl+k and ctrl+j
nnoremap <C-k> <C-a>
nnoremap <C-j> <C-x>
