Have you ever had to retype a whole number just to increment or decrement it by one or a few unites? I'll show you a better way to do this. Vim has a built-in simple command in Normal Mode for adding and subtracting the number under the cursor by one unite: Ctrl+a and Ctrl+x.

This commands really shines when you prescende it with the number of times you want it to repeat. So, to increment a number under the cursor by 10, you just type 10 Ctrl+a. And to decrement it by 22, you just type 22 Ctrl+x.

[ gif demonstration ]

Now, if you use Tmux with the Ctrl+a keybind — or you just find the default Vim keybind for this not intuitive enough — I have a suggestion for you: use Ctrl+j for decrement and Ctrl+k for increment.
